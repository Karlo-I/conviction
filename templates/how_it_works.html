How It Works & AI Disclosure

The platform

Conviction helps you trace the line between daily experiences (rising costs, poor health, environmental damage) and the systemic mechanisms that produce those outcomes. You explore three lenses — Food, Housing, and Mobility — and discover the cross-domain forces beneath them all.

AI Agent disclosure

Conviction uses an AI agent (Claude Haiku by Anthropic) to assist with peer validation. Here's exactly what it does:

What the AI does

  • Generates evidence digests: When you submit a contribution, the AI fetches data from pre-approved sources (WHO, World Bank, Our World in Data) and summarizes what the evidence shows relative to your claim
  • Presents findings, not verdicts: The AI reports what it found — it does not approve or reject contributions
  • Surfaces divergence: Where your source conflicts with institutional data, the AI explicitly shows that conflict rather than resolving it
  • Runs once per contribution: The AI generates one digest shown to all validators — it does not run separately for each validator

What the AI does NOT do

  • Make moderation decisions
  • Name or blame individuals or organizations
  • Fetch data from URLs you provide (validators manually check source links)
  • Replace human judgment — validators make the final decision

Limitations

  • Geographic coverage: WHO and World Bank data is more complete for OECD countries. Where data is absent, the AI reports "no data available"
  • Reference sources: Institutional datasets are used as reference points for comparison, not as absolute arbiters of truth
  • Model: We use Claude Haiku (claude-haiku-4-5-20251001) — a lightweight, fast model optimized for summarization

Peer validation

All contributions go through peer review. Validators read the AI digest and the original source, then vote to approve or reject. Two approvals are needed before a contribution appears on the platform.

Transparency

Conviction has a thesis and does not pretend otherwise. What we offer instead of neutrality is full transparency: every data point sourced and cited, every editorial decision documented, every methodology open to scrutiny.
