How It Works

Transparency about the technology, AI, and methodology behind Conviction.

The Diagnostic Quiz

The quiz classifies your lived experience into one of three systemic lenses: Food, Housing, or Mobility. It uses a weighted scoring system to determine which domain you feel most directly. This is not machine learning — it's a rule-based classification that matches your responses to pre-defined patterns.

Retake policy: You can retake the quiz once every 90 days. Your circumstances change; your lens might too.

Token Economy

When you register, you receive 10 tokens. These are spent on issues within each lens to signal conviction — a deliberate act that forces genuine prioritisation, unlike a passive "like" button.

Earning tokens:

  • Contributing local data points (pending peer validation)
  • Validating other users' contributions (1 token per validation)
  • Sharing content (rate-limited to prevent gaming)

AI Contribution Digest

When you submit a contribution, an AI agent generates a plain-language summary comparing your claim against pre-approved data sources. This digest is shown to all peer validators.

How the AI works:

  1. Constructs a search query from your claim, country, and indicator
  2. Fetches data from pre-approved sources (WHO, World Bank, FAO, Our World in Data)
  3. Sends the data + your claim to a lightweight language model (Claude Haiku or GPT-4o Mini)
  4. Generates a summary of what the evidence says relative to your claim — not a verdict
  5. Returns a confidence signal: "Evidence found", "Partial evidence", or "No data available"

Important principles:

  • The AI presents evidence. Humans decide. The AI never approves or rejects contributions — that power remains with peer validators.
  • Pre-approved sources are reference points, not arbiters of truth. Where your submitted source diverges from institutional data, the digest surfaces that divergence explicitly. The conflict is the insight.
  • The AI runs once per contribution. All validators see the same output. This keeps costs proportional to contributions submitted, not user count.
  • Cost per contribution: Approximately $0.002–$0.01 using lightweight models.

Limitations:

  • Coverage is uneven globally. WHO and World Bank data is more complete for OECD countries. Where data is absent, the digest says "No data available" explicitly — we flag this rather than hide it.
  • The AI cannot fetch URLs. You must paste source text for the AI to process. The URL you provide is for validators to verify manually.
  • The AI is not infallible. It can misinterpret context or miss nuance. Validators are expected to read both the digest and your original source.

Peer Validation

Contributions enter a validation queue where other users review the AI digest and your submitted evidence. A contribution requires multiple approvals before being added to the platform.

Validators cannot add new evidence — they can only approve or reject based on what you submitted. This is a deliberate scope decision. If you know of a relevant source, submit it as your own contribution.

The Forces Layer

The forces layer surfaces cross-domain mechanisms — financial capture, regulatory arbitrage, externalised cost, information asymmetry — that operate across Food, Housing, and Mobility simultaneously.

Community-built, not editor-curated. Users submit force claims through the same contribution flow. These require an elevated validation threshold (more approvals than regular contributions) before being elevated to the forces layer.

Cross-lens validation: For a force claim to be elevated, it must be linked to at least two different lenses. A mechanism that only operates in one domain is an issue, not a force.

Privacy by Design

We do not collect email addresses. This is a deliberate privacy-by-design decision. You register with a username and password only.

Account recovery is not possible. If you lose your password, we cannot recover your account. Choose credentials you will remember.

Data deletion: You can request full account deletion at any time. Your contributions and votes are retained in anonymised form to preserve evidence integrity; all other personal data is deleted within 30 days.

Technology Stack

  • Backend: Python / Flask
  • Database: SQLite (development) → PostgreSQL (production)
  • Frontend: HTML, CSS, Vanilla JavaScript (no framework)
  • Mapping: Leaflet.js
  • Charts: Chart.js
  • AI Models: Claude Haiku (Anthropic) / GPT-4o Mini (OpenAI)
  • Hosting: Render (free tier)

Echo Chamber Prevention

A platform built around systemic critique will attract users who already agree. We actively counter confirmation bias through architectural choices:

  • Quiz cross-pollination: After routing you to your primary lens, we surface a secondary lens with a specific data point as the hook.
  • Heatmap "least heard" mode: Toggle between "highest conviction" (where token spend is densest) and "least heard" (countries and issues with real data but low token spend).
  • Cross-lens force links: Seeing that the same force operates across domains pulls you out of silos by evidence structure, not algorithmic recommendation.
  • Dissenting data shown explicitly: Where pre-approved API data complicates or contradicts the platform's thesis, that data is displayed — not hidden.

Open Methodology

Every aspect of this platform is documented and auditable:

  • All data sources are cited with methodology links
  • AI prompts and model choices are documented
  • Validation thresholds are configurable without code changes
  • The entire codebase is open source

We have a thesis. What we offer instead of neutrality is full transparency.
